# Edmonton Climate Reference (Zone 4a)

## Frost & season
- Average last spring frost: **May 7** (frost has occurred as late as early June — watch forecasts through May)
- Conventional safe planting for tender annuals: **May long weekend (~May 18)**
- Average first fall frost: **Sept 22** (can arrive in early September)
- Frost-free season: ~135–140 days
- Growing degree days (base 5°C): ~1,400–1,600

July is the wettest month but also peak ET — supplemental watering is still usually needed.

## Edmonton-specific hazards
- **Hail:** June–August. Central Alberta is in hail alley. Forecast severe-thunderstorm
  watches should trigger a "move pots under cover" alert.
- **Late spring frost:** Tender transplants out before May 18 need frost cloth on standby.
- **Early fall frost:** A single early-September frost night often precedes 2–3 more weeks
  of good growing weather — covering plants that one night extends the season meaningfully.
- **Winter chinook/freeze-thaw:** Mulch perennials *after* ground freezes (early Nov) to keep
  ground frozen, not to keep it warm. Snow cover is the best insulator.
- **Drying winter winds:** Evergreens and tender shrubs benefit from burlap screens and a
  deep late-October watering before freeze-up.

## Soil
- Typical Edmonton soil: alkaline (pH 7.5–8.0), often heavy clay in newer neighbourhoods.
- Acid-loving plants (blueberries, azaleas, some hydrangea colours) struggle without major amendment.
- Clay holds water — overwatering is as common a failure as underwatering. The watering
  model accounts for soil_type per zone.

## Fertilizer calendar (general)
- **Mid-May:** first feed for perennials/shrubs as growth starts
- **May–July:** vegetables on their per-plant interval
- **Aug 1:** STOP nitrogen on perennials, shrubs, trees — late feeding produces soft
  growth that winter-kills
- **Late Sept:** fall lawn fertilizer (high K) only

## Common Edmonton pests (watch windows)
| Pest | Window | First response |
|---|---|---|
| Aphids | Jun–Aug | Water blast, insecticidal soap, ladybugs |
| Flea beetles | May–Jun | Row cover on brassicas/transplants |
| Cabbage moth/worm | Jun–Aug | BTK spray, row cover |
| Delia (root maggot) | May–Jun | Collars, row cover |
| Slugs | wet spells | Beer traps, diatomaceous earth |
| Red lily beetle | May–Jul | Hand-pick daily |
| Sawfly (on roses/currants) | Jun | Hand-pick, soap spray |
| Black knot (fungal, on cherry/plum/mayday) | year-round | Prune out 15–20 cm below knot, winter |
